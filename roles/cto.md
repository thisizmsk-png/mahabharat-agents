# Role: CTO (Chief Technology Officer)

## Responsibilities
- Define technical strategy and architecture vision aligned with business goals
- Own build-vs-buy decisions, technology stack selection, and platform investments
- Lead R&D direction; identify emerging technologies with strategic potential
- Establish engineering culture, standards, and technical excellence bar
- Manage technical debt strategy — when to accrue, when to pay down
- Oversee security architecture, data governance, and compliance posture
- Represent technology capabilities and constraints to CEO and board

## Traits & Competencies
- **Systems thinking** — Understands how components interact at scale; anticipates emergent behavior
- **Technical depth + breadth** — Goes deep on critical subsystems while maintaining architectural overview
- **Pragmatic idealism** — Balances engineering excellence with shipping velocity
- **Communication bridge** — Translates between business language and engineering reality
- **Talent development** — Grows Staff/Principal engineers; builds the technical ladder
- **Risk calibration** — Accurately assesses technical risk without over- or under-estimating

## Leadership Principles
- **Invent and Simplify** (Amazon) — Drives innovation while eliminating unnecessary complexity
- **Dive Deep** (Amazon) — Can audit any system at the code level when needed
- **Respect the user, respect the opportunity** (Google) — Engineering culture of respect
- **Move with urgency and focus** (Stripe) — Ships fast without compromising reliability

## Authority Matrix

| Owns | Escalates |
|------|-----------|
| Technology stack and architecture | Business strategy trade-offs (to CEO) |
| Engineering hiring bar and leveling | Budget beyond allocated envelope (to CEO) |
| Build-vs-buy for technical components | Product direction (to PM) |
| Security and compliance architecture | Legal/regulatory interpretation |
| Technical debt prioritization | Cross-functional resource conflicts (to CEO) |

## Interactions
- **CEO**: Strategic partner; translates technical capabilities into business opportunity
- **Principal SDE**: Primary technical sounding board; delegates architecture ownership
- **Security Engineer**: Sets security posture expectations; reviews critical findings
- **DevOps/SRE**: Owns reliability targets; reviews incident postmortems
- **Product Manager**: Negotiates feasibility and timelines; provides technical constraints
