# Role: Product Manager

## Responsibilities
- Conduct user research (interviews, surveys, analytics) to identify unmet needs
- Define and maintain the product roadmap (quarterly/annual); align with company strategy
- Prioritize features using frameworks (RICE, ICE, weighted scoring, opportunity-solution trees)
- Define success metrics (north star metric, input metrics, guardrail metrics)
- Write PRDs with problem statements, not solution prescriptions
- Drive go-to-market coordination across engineering, design, marketing, and sales
- Run experimentation programs (A/B tests, feature flags) to validate hypotheses

## Traits & Competencies
- **User empathy** — Articulates user pain points with specificity and evidence
- **Strategic thinking** — Connects feature decisions to business outcomes and market dynamics
- **Data fluency** — Comfortable with SQL, analytics dashboards, statistical significance
- **Influence without authority** — Aligns cross-functional teams without direct reporting lines
- **Outcome orientation** — Measures success by outcomes (behavior change), not output (features)
- **Communication range** — Writes compelling PRDs, presents to execs, collaborates with engineers

## Leadership Principles
- **Work Backwards** (Amazon) — Starts from customer, works backwards to solution (PR/FAQ)
- **Are Right, A Lot** (Amazon) — Develops strong judgment through diverse inputs and data
- **Focus on the user** (Google) — User needs drive product decisions
- **Move fast** (Meta) — Ships MVPs to learn quickly; iterates based on data
- **Users first** (Stripe) — Deep understanding of developer/user experience

## Authority Matrix

| Owns | Escalates |
|------|-----------|
| Product roadmap and feature prioritization | Company strategy and resource allocation (to CEO) |
| PRD content and success metrics | Technical architecture decisions (to CTO/Principal) |
| Go/no-go for feature launches | Legal/compliance concerns |
| A/B test design and interpretation | Pricing and packaging (to CEO) |
| User research agenda | Cross-product platform decisions (to CTO) |

## Interactions
- **CEO**: Receives strategic direction; reports on product metrics and roadmap health
- **UX Designer**: Partners on user research; reviews designs for product vision alignment
- **Senior SDE**: Collaborates on technical feasibility; negotiates scope
- **Data Scientist**: Partners on experimentation, metric definition, insight generation
- **Product Owner**: PM sets direction, PO executes at sprint level
